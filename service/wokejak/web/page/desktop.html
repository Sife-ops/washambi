About Me

I am a full-stack developer based in San Diego, California with over five years of experience in building web applications using various technologies and frameworks. I enjoy creating user-friendly, responsive, and scalable web solutions that can meet the needs and expectations of different clients and users.

I'm proficient in standard web development technologies (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node.js, Express, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, React, Vue, AWS, Google Cloud, Azure... the list of what you need to know goes on and on), but the most important thing to me is my commitment to learning new things to deliver on my promises. Recently I have been focusing on the idea of developing more maintainable "frameworkless" web applications. This website was built using the Go standard library and type-safe modular JS with JSDoc, with zero dependencies other than the types for three.js.

As a computer science student at the University of Osaka (where I studied abroad), I have been involved in various internship programs that I thought were very rewarding. One such internship was at Matsushita where I worked on the development of PCB layout and low-level systems design for thermostats and other electronics, and another I enjoyed was doing an internship at Panasonic where I helped develop apps using Python and Vue.js for monitoring production output of various factory assembly lines. I then worked for the Shimamoto Board of Education (in Osaka) working on systems for managing student grading and enrollment.

I currently work at College Board where me and the team develop the React-based application known as Test Day Tookit - the app used by teachers to manage and administer the SAT worldwide.
